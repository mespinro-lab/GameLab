Three critical blockers found: food data corruption in event resolution, a succession chain softlock when health reaches 0, and a silent full-game progression blocker (universal techs gated behind a player button that can be ignored forever). Seven major issues include an unguarded debug tool that trivializes the core prototype test, two immortality paths that neutralize the health clock, and sub-minimum touch targets on the game's primary interaction elements. ## S1 — Critical (block release)

### FOOD-01 · Food goes negative in event discovery resolution
- **Reported by**: playtester-tycoon
- **Source**: `game.js:356`
- **Description**: `resolveDiscoveryOption` applies `state.food += opt.food_delta` with no `Math.max(0, ...)` clamp. Every other food mutation in the codebase is clamped. Event `ev_desc_figuretes` uses `food_delta: -3`; if the player has food=1 when it fires, `state.food` becomes −2.
- **Reproduction**: (1) Let food drain to 1 via upkeep. (2) Execute `act_ritual_foc` or `act_gran_ritual` until `ev_desc_figuretes` fires from `pool_social`. (3) Choose "Demanar-li que t'ensenyi a fer-ne (−3 Aliment)". Food becomes −2.
- **Impact**: Invariant `food ≥ 0` violated. Display shows negative food (looks like a bug to players). The next action's `Math.max(0, state.food - FOOD_UPKEEP)` clamp masks the corruption rather than preventing it.
- **Fix direction**: `state.food = Math.max(0, state.food + opt.food_delta)` at `game.js:356`.
- **Route to**: `gameplay-programmer`

### SUCC-01 · Health and food not reset on succession — chain game-over when health triggers it
- **Reported by**: playtester-tycoon
- **Source**: `game.js:395–425` (`continueSuccession`)
- **Description**: `continueSuccession` resets `cycle = 0` and creates a new character, but never resets `state.health` or `state.food`. When succession is triggered by `health ≤ 0` (the `game.js:329` check), the new generation begins at health=0. The very first action fires the succession check again → finds `hasChildren = false` (brand-new character) → `no_heir` game over. The game loops into an inescapable chain.
- **Reproduction**: (1) Execute actions with `health_delta: -2` (e.g. `act_cacera_gran`) repeatedly until health ≤ 0. (2) Succession fires. (3) Click "Continua". (4) New generation triggers immediate game over — `no_heir`.
- **Impact**: Progression blocker for any health-driven succession. Players who take risky actions in gen 1+ will experience an unrecoverable loop with no way to restart the dynasty.
- **Fix direction**: Add `state.health = HEALTH_MAX; state.food = STARTING_FOOD;` (or a partial restore) inside `continueSuccession` before creating the new character.
- **Route to**: `gameplay-programmer`

### GATE-01 · Universal techs require a player button click — permanently lockable by inaction
- **Reported by**: playtester-speed-runner
- **Source**: `game.js` (`getDiscoverableTechs`, `discoverTech`, universal-techs-section button)
- **Description**: Universal techs become *available* at their cycle threshold, but are only applied when the player clicks "Descobrir." Nothing forces or auto-applies them. A player who ignores the button never populates `state.discoveredUniversalTechIds`. All 6 branch techs have a `universal_prereq` field; `getEligibleBranchTechs()` requires that prereq to be discovered before a tech is eligible. If no universal techs are clicked, no branch techs are ever eligible, `getEligibleBranchTechs().length === 0`, and `act_escoltar_estrangers` (the discovery action) never appears in any zone card. The player can reach cycle 14 with zero branch tech access and no error, no warning, and no understanding of why nothing is unlocking.
- **Reproduction**: (1) Play an entire generation. (2) Never click any "Descobrir" button. (3) All zone cards show only base actions; discovery notification never appears; all 6 branch techs remain locked.
- **Impact**: Silent full-game progression blocker. Branch techs, destresa via discovery events, and all second-tier content are inaccessible. The prototype's core loop cannot be evaluated by a player who misses the button.
- **Fix direction**: Either auto-apply `discoverTech(tech.id)` when `state.cycle` reaches the threshold (inside `executeAction` or a dedicated cycle-advance hook), or display a modal / forced notification that cannot be dismissed until the player clicks "Descobrir".
- **Route to**: `gameplay-programmer` (implementation); `game-designer` (confirm auto vs. manual intent)

## S2 — Major (fix before milestone)

### SABER-01 · Saber (materials) not reset on succession — cross-generation resource exploit
- **Reported by**: playtester-optimizer
- **Source**: `game.js:395–425` (`continueSuccession`)
- **Description**: `state.materials` (the Saber/🧠 currency) is not reset when a new generation begins. Resources accumulated across any number of generations carry forward. Total cost to purchase all purchasable actions ≈ 62 Saber. A player who spends gen 1 and gen 2 farming `act_tallar_pedra` (~2 Saber/cycle) can accumulate ≈42–62 Saber and unlock everything in gen 3's opening cycles, collapsing the purchase progression entirely.
- **Reproduction**: (1) Gen 1: spam `act_tallar_pedra`. (2) Reach succession without spending Saber. (3) Gen 2: repeat. (4) Gen 3: buy every action immediately.
- **Impact**: Economic loop (earn Saber → buy actions → unlock content) is bypassed from gen 2 onward for players who notice the carry-over.
- **Fix direction**: Reset `state.materials = 0` in `continueSuccession`, or implement a capped inheritance (e.g. 5–10 Saber as "tribal knowledge").
- **Route to**: `gameplay-programmer`

### DISC-01 · Discovery action advances cycle without applying upkeep
- **Reported by**: playtester-tycoon · playtester-speed-runner
- **Source**: `game.js:182–198` (`performDiscoveryAction`)
- **Description**: `performDiscoveryAction` calls `state.cycle++` and checks for succession, but never deducts `FOOD_UPKEEP` or `HEALTH_UPKEEP`. Every other cycle-advancing code path (via `executeAction`) always deducts both. A player who has multiple branch techs eligible can unlock them in rapid succession with zero survival cost.
- **Reproduction**: (1) Satisfy conditions for several branch techs simultaneously. (2) Execute "Escoltar els Estrangers" multiple times. (3) Cycles advance, branch techs unlock, food and health do not change.
- **Impact**: Survival pressure (the game's core tension) is completely bypassed during discovery. Discovery cycles are effectively free.
- **Fix direction**: Add `state.food = Math.max(0, state.food - FOOD_UPKEEP); state.health = Math.max(0, state.health - HEALTH_UPKEEP);` inside `performDiscoveryAction` after the cycle increment.
- **Route to**: `gameplay-programmer`

### EVENT-01 · is_single_use discovery events repeat indefinitely if declined
- **Reported by**: playtester-tycoon · playtester-optimizer
- **Source**: `game.js:200–211` (`getEligiblePoolEvents`); `data.js:315–331` (discovery events)
- **Description**: Five discovery events are marked `is_single_use: true` in data.js. `getEligiblePoolEvents` filters by "already discovered" but never checks `is_single_use` and never tracks which events have fired. If the player chooses the decline option (`discovers: false`), the event is not added to `unlockedBranchTechIds`, so it remains eligible and re-enters the pool on every subsequent draw from that pool.
- **Reproduction**: (1) Execute `act_espiar_ramat` and receive `ev_desc_llancador`. (2) Choose "Seguir el teu camí" (decline). (3) Execute `act_espiar_ramat` again. (4) Same event fires again. Repeatable indefinitely.
- **Impact**: Discovery events repeat with the same narrative text, breaking immersion. The decline option also grants a small food bonus (+1–2) each time, creating a minor farming path.
- **Fix direction**: Add `state.character.firedSingleUseEventIds = new Set()` to character state; populate it on event fire (both discover and decline); filter it in `getEligiblePoolEvents`.
- **Route to**: `gameplay-programmer`

### FADE-01 · FADE_MARGIN is 0.10 in code; spec says 0.05
- **Reported by**: playtester-tycoon
- **Source**: `game.js:10`
- **Description**: `const FADE_MARGIN = 0.10` — double the intended value. This widens the "ghost zone" around every inclination threshold: actions that should be HIDDEN (player clearly not heading toward them) appear FADED instead, implying they are reachable with a small push.
- **Reproduction**: Set impuls to 0.0. `act_cacera_gran` requires `impuls ≥ 0.10`. With FADE_MARGIN=0.10: `0.0 >= 0.10 - 0.10 = 0.0` → FADED. With correct 0.05: `0.0 >= 0.05` → false → HIDDEN. The action is incorrectly shown.
- **Impact**: Balance and UX design around inclination thresholds is undermined. Players see false affordance on actions that their build cannot reach.
- **Fix direction**: Change `FADE_MARGIN = 0.10` to `FADE_MARGIN = 0.05` at `game.js:10`. Confirm intended value with game-designer first.
- **Route to**: `game-designer` (confirm intended value) → `gameplay-programmer` (change constant)

### TOUCH-01 · Zone filter tabs and action buttons below 44px touch minimum on mobile
- **Reported by**: playtester-casual-mobile
- **Source**: `style.css` (`.zone-filter-btn`, `.btn-small`)
- **Description**: Zone filter tabs (Actives / Aprendre / Bloq.) use `padding: 5px 4px; font-size: 10px` → ~20px rendered height. `Executar` / `Aprendre` action buttons use `.btn-small` (`padding: 4px 10px; font-size: 11px`) → ~19px rendered height. The global `button { min-height: 44px }` override at the 600px breakpoint applies, but without `align-items: center` and explicit padding override on `.zone-filter-btn`, the visual and perceived tap area remains tiny. Both elements are below the Apple HIG 44px minimum; the filter tabs are the most-tapped element in the game.
- **Reproduction**: Load at 360px viewport width. Attempt to tap filter tabs or Executar buttons one-handed.
- **Impact**: Core game interaction (switching tabs, executing actions) is miss-tap-prone on any mobile device. Casual players will experience constant misfires on the primary loop.
- **Fix direction**: Add `min-height: 44px; display: flex; align-items: center; justify-content: center;` explicitly to `.zone-filter-btn` and `.btn-small` in the mobile breakpoint.
- **Route to**: `ui-programmer`

### IMMO-01 · Two independent paths to permanent health immunity
- **Reported by**: playtester-optimizer
- **Source**: `data.js` (`act_gran_ritual`, `act_curar_grup`)
- **Description**: (Path A) `act_gran_ritual` (upgrade, no inclination requirement, 4 Saber) has `health_delta: +2`. Net per cycle: +2 − 1 upkeep = **+1 health**. Health permanently stays at 19–20. (Path B) `act_curar_grup` (requires `bt_curar_herbes`, 3 Saber) outputs 3–6 health; at max vincle stat (5.0) + `d_guaridor` destresa: ~8 health/cycle vs. 1 upkeep. Two completely separate mechanics each independently neutralize the health clock.
- **Impact**: Health pressure — the only aging mechanic driving succession urgency — is defeated by a single upgrade accessible early with no inclination requirement. The game's survival tension disappears from gen 1.
- **Fix direction**: Cap `health_delta` healing to net 0 (restore only what upkeep takes), or gate healing behind a build cost (inclination requirement, diminishing returns, or cooldown).
- **Route to**: `economy-designer`

### DEBUG-01 · Inclination dot editor live in player-facing UI — trivializes entire prototype test
- **Reported by**: playtester-tycoon · playtester-optimizer
- **Source**: `game.js:1077–1083`
- **Description**: Clicking any inclination dot in the profile panel directly sets `state.character.inclination[axis] = INCL_DOT_VALUES[idx]`, bypassing `applyDelta` and all inertia. No guard, no `DEBUG_MODE` flag, no UI label. Any player opening the prototype can click dots to instantly set any axis to ±0.5 or ±1.0, unlock any branch, and skip every progression gate.
- **Impact**: The prototype's central question — "does building inclination through action feel engaging?" — cannot be answered honestly if players can override inclinations with one click. All S2 and S3 exploit findings above assume this tool is NOT used; with it, every exploit is trivial in 0 cycles.
- **Fix direction**: Gate behind a `DEBUG_MODE = false` constant flag, or move to a hidden dev panel (e.g. `Ctrl+Shift+D`) not visible in the standard UI.
- **Route to**: `qa-lead` / `game-designer` (confirm scope for this prototype); `gameplay-programmer` (implement flag)

### DYN-01 · Hunter dynasty loses Caçador branch identity by gen 3 under realistic play

Branch identity is lost silently at succession with no player warning. The succession modal shows the top axis value but does not compare it to branch thresholds.

- **Reported by**: playtester-dynasty-builder
- Terminal generation (gen 5) ends with a plain game-over modal ("Cinc generacions han passat...") with no summary of the dynasty's accumulated stats, inclinations, branch techs, or destresas. The succession modal is also skipped for gen 5. All dynasty data is discarded silently.

### OPT-01 · Inclination axis reversal is mathematically impossible in 14 cycles

## Loop Hypothesis

> Core question: "Does inclination → branch → action → succession feel natural?"

**Verdict: PARTIAL**

Evidence FOR: Branch techs and purchased actions are correctly inherited across all 5 generations (no decay, proper Set cloning). Inertia system (INERTIA_FACTOR=2.0) correctly prevents runaway axis dominance in normal play. The bt_agulla_os unlock route via `tallar_pedra ×9` is mechanically coherent and satisfying. Data cross-references are clean (all action IDs, event pool IDs, and universal prereqs are valid).

Evidence AGAINST: Three S1 bugs block normal play before the loop can be experienced honestly. Universal tech gate (GATE-01) is the deepest break — a player who misses the button never sees branch techs at all. Healing immortality (IMMO-01) removes the urgency that drives succession decisions. Saber carry-over (SABER-01) removes economic tension from gen 2+. At the design level: branch identity decays below threshold by gen 3, destresa lock-in prevents dynasty evolution, and stats provide no meaningful compound advantage.

## Recommended Next Actions

1. **Fix S1 trio first** (`gameplay-programmer`): FOOD-01 (one-line clamp at `game.js:356`), SUCC-01 (add health/food reset in `continueSuccession`), GATE-01 (auto-apply universal techs on cycle advance or force a modal). These three together block honest play of the core loop.

2. **Gate the dot editor** (`gameplay-programmer` + `qa-lead`): Add `DEBUG_MODE = false` constant and guard the inclination dot-click listener. Without this, playtest data from any informal session is meaningless — players can trivially skip every system.

3. **Fix Saber carry-over + discovery upkeep** (`gameplay-programmer`): SABER-01 and DISC-01 both collapse economic pressure from gen 2 onward. Two focused fixes in `continueSuccession` and `performDiscoveryAction`.

4. **Fix EVENT-01** (`gameplay-programmer`): Track fired single-use events; filter in `getEligiblePoolEvents`. Repeated discovery narratives destroy immersion on every playtest session.

5. **Design review: branch thresholds and inheritance rate** (`game-designer` + `economy-designer`): With S1 fixes in place, run a targeted dynasty sweep. Consider raising BRANCH_INHERITANCE_RATE to 0.80 for inclinations, recalibrating branch thresholds to match realistic inherited values, and decaying 1 destresa slot on succession. These are the tuning decisions that determine whether the dynasty fantasy is achievable.

6. **Mobile UX pass** (`ui-programmer` + `ux-designer`): TOUCH-01 (filter tabs + buttons), NP-01/03/05/06 copy changes, and the succession modal "stats" → "atributs" rename are a coherent UX batch that can be addressed in one session.
